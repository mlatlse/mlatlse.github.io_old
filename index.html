About




Set up in 2017 by students returning on exchange from UC Berkeley, the LSESU Machine Learning Society (ML@LSE) aims to increase the LSE communityâ€™s general understanding of machine learning techniques, its potential applications and implications for society. We also hope to equip members with the basic competencies and technical knowledge necessary for implementing machine learning models.






Activities

Bootcamps

Since our inaugural bootcamp on Suppor Vector Machines on on 1st November 2017, we have conducted several bootcamps on Random Forests, Principles of Machine Learning, Neural Networks. We cover both theory and implementation on Jupyter notebooks. Learn about the state of the art methods used in Machine Learning and their applications to Economics. No previous programming experience needed!

Projects

Work with a team of motivated students to design Machine Learning solutions for industry projects and research projects. Previous projects include sentiment analysis of tweets relating to stocks, and designing a mini-financial advisory chatbot. The 2018-19 Committee has pioneered an industry mentorship programme with Datatonic - see below!

Talks

Come to our speaker talks where we invite industry practitioners and leading academics to discuss the latest advances in Machine Learning and interesting applications in industry. Great opportunities to network with like minded students and understand potential career opportunities in industry.

Projects



We are proud to announce our Industry Mentorship Programme with Datatonic, a London-based data team leveraging cutting-edge machine learning to help businesses unlock greater performance. Stay tuned to this space for updates!
